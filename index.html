A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Former President of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam (Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam) was a renowned Indian scientist, educator, and the 11th President of India (2002–2007). Widely known as the "People's President", he earned this title due to his humble beginnings and deep connection with the Indian people. Dr. Kalam's remarkable contributions to India's aerospace, defence technology, and nuclear and missile programs left a lasting legacy.

  1. APJ Abdul Kalam’s full name is Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam .
  2. He was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, on October 15th, 1931.
  3. Kalam had been selling newspapers to support the income of his family.
  4. In 1960, he studied aeronautical engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.
  5. His dream as a child was to become a fighter pilot
  6. After graduation, he entered the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist.
  7. In 1969, he joined the Organization of Indian Space Research (ISRO).
  8. Kalam Sir served as the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle III Project Director (PSLV III).
  9. His expertise in space science made him known as 'Missile Man of India’
  10. He was the 11th President of India. On July 27th, 2015, he took his last breath.
